a different view on Cologne

About

A city is a complex collection of structures, these structures put together create their own image, which shapes the city and gives it its character. Some of these structures appear in a pattern so characteristic of the city that this individual structure already shows which city it is. In the case of Cologne, these significant structures are primarily traffic, parks and green areas as well as buildings.

The construction of the old town and its defensive structures, in a star-shaped ring directly next to the Rhine, is fundamental to this distinctive form of structure.

The city of Cologne has continued to grow in this form and has been mostey retained in its expansion.

Apart from the fact that these structures reflect Cologne in their own recognizable form, they also reflect more information about Cologne. At the same time, these structures are also very strongly contrasted, which makes it very interesting to compare them.

There would be traffic primarily as transport between point A and point B and parks/green areas predominantly for leisure and relaxation. Example Ebertplatz 

Unlike Barbarossa Square, there is no such radical distribution of structures. No structure has been repressed by others or has disappeared completely. There is a large amount of green space in addition to the fact that the traffic area is relatively balanced between pedestrians and road traffic. But what becomes very clear is a separation of pedestrian areas; pedestrian area and also green areas. The traffic area cuts off the square and parking area from the remaining areas with multi-lane roads.
